SCENARIO
  Date/time : 2025-04-10, approximately 09:45 UTC
  Target    : Finance employee at Nordik Bank (SIMULATED entity)
  Attacker  : Posing as "IT Security Helpdesk" -- Mark Schreiber
  Goal      : Steal One-Time Password (OTP) in real time to bypass MFA

==============================================================
CALL TRANSCRIPT
==============================================================

ATTACKER:
  "Good morning. This is Mark Schreiber calling from the IT Security
   Helpdesk. Could I please speak with [EMPLOYEE NAME]?"

EMPLOYEE:
  "Speaking."

ATTACKER:
  "Thank you. I'm calling because our security monitoring system has
   flagged repeated failed login attempts against your account over
   the last 30 minutes, originating from an IP address in Eastern
   Europe. We believe your credentials may have been compromised.
   Are you currently at your workstation?"

EMPLOYEE:
  "Yes, I am."

ATTACKER:
  "Good. To protect your account we need to re-validate your identity
   right now. I've already blocked the suspicious IP, but I need you
   to log into the staff VPN portal -- the address is
   vpn-portal.nordik-internal.example -- and tell me the 6-digit code
   that appears on screen after you authenticate. I'll use that on my
   end to push a security patch to your session token."

  [Attacker enters the stolen OTP into the real portal in real time,
   gaining access to the employee's account before the OTP expires.]

ATTACKER (after OTP is entered):
  "Perfect, thank you. I've applied the patch. You may see a brief
   session refresh. Your account is now secured. Please don't share
   today's support ticket number with anyone -- it's part of an
   active security investigation. Have a good day."

==============================================================
SOCIAL ENGINEERING TECHNIQUES (for debrief):

1. AUTHORITY -- IT Security impersonation, professional tone
2. URGENCY -- active attack in progress, immediate action required
3. FEAR -- account compromise, Eastern Europe IP (credible threat)
4. PRETEXT -- plausible cover story (VPN session patch)
5. OTP RELAY ATTACK -- real-time MFA bypass (T1111)
6. SECRECY -- "don't share the ticket number" prevents verification

MITRE ATT&CK MAPPING:
  T1566      -- Phishing (voice variant)
  T1111      -- Multi-Factor Authentication Interception
  T1078      -- Valid Accounts (credential abuse post-OTP theft)

INDICATORS OF VISHING:
  - Unsolicited inbound call claiming to be IT Support
  - Caller knows employee name (from LinkedIn / earlier recon)
  - Request for OTP / password over the phone
  - Pressure to act immediately, not to verify via other channels
  - Plausible but unverifiable cover story

STUDENT DISCUSSION POINTS:
  Q1: What policy would prevent this attack?
      (Never share OTPs verbally; IT never asks for them)
  Q2: How could the employee verify the caller's identity?
      (Hang up, call the published IT helpdesk number independently)
  Q3: What telemetry would show this attack in Wazuh/SIEM?
      (Login from new IP + MFA success after employee call started)
