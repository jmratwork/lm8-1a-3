SCENARIO TITLE: "Operation Locked Ledger"

BACKGROUND
  Nordik Bank (SIMULATED entity) operates 12 branch offices and a central
  data centre. On Monday morning at 06:15 UTC, the IT Service Desk begins
  receiving calls from branch staff unable to access shared drives.
  By 06:30, it is clear that a ransomware incident is underway.

  You are the Incident Response team. The Incident Commander has called
  an emergency bridge. It is now 06:45 UTC.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
INJECT 1 – 06:45 UTC: INITIAL ASSESSMENT
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Known facts at 06:45 UTC:
  - File servers FS-01 through FS-08 are OFFLINE or showing encrypted files
  - File extension .locked is visible on 4 of 8 servers (partial encryption)
  - FS-09 through FS-12 appear unaffected
  - A ransom note "README_RESTORE.txt" has been found on affected shares
  - Ransom demand: 12 BTC (~EUR 480,000 at current rate) within 48 hours
  - Backups: last known-good backup is 2025-11-08 (7 days old)
  - Unknown whether data was exfiltrated before encryption

DISCUSSION QUESTIONS – INJECT 1:
  a) What are your IMMEDIATE containment actions (first 15 minutes)?
  b) Who needs to be notified RIGHT NOW (internal + external)?
  c) Do you isolate ALL servers including the apparently unaffected FS-09–12?
     What are the trade-offs?
  d) What evidence do you preserve before taking systems offline?

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
INJECT 2 – 07:30 UTC: SCOPE EXPANDS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

New information at 07:30 UTC:
  - Forensics team finds ransomware binary on ALL 12 file servers;
    FS-09–12 were infected but encryption had not yet started
  - The ransomware operator's leak site (found via TOR) lists Nordik Bank
    and claims to have exfiltrated 2.4 TB of customer data
  - The initial access vector appears to be a phishing email sent to 3 staff
    on 2025-11-08 (7 days ago); one user opened a malicious attachment
  - Legal is asking: do we have a GDPR notification obligation? Timeline?

DISCUSSION QUESTIONS – INJECT 2:
  a) Does the threat actor's leak-site claim change your decision on paying?
  b) What is the GDPR notification obligation? (72-hour clock from what point?)
  c) What data classification did the exfiltrated files likely contain?
  d) The attacker had 7 days of dwell time. What could they have done besides
     deploy ransomware? (persistence, further lateral movement, data staging)
  e) Revise your containment perimeter in light of this information.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
INJECT 3 – 10:00 UTC: MANAGEMENT DECISION POINT
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Management brings three options to the team:
  Option A: Pay the ransom (BTC payment, possible decryption key)
  Option B: Restore from backup (7-day data loss; ~72h estimated recovery)
  Option C: Hybrid – begin restoration while legal evaluates payment as leverage

Additional context:
  - Cyber insurance policy has a EUR 250,000 sublimit for ransomware payments
  - The decryption tool provided by ransomware groups works in ~65% of cases
  - Branch operations without file servers = EUR ~85,000/day lost revenue
  - Customer-facing online banking is NOT affected (separate infrastructure)
  - Regulatory authority (ECB/BaFin equivalent) has been informally notified

DISCUSSION QUESTIONS – INJECT 3:
  a) What is your recommendation to management? Argue for your choice.
  b) What factors would change your recommendation?
  c) What do you tell the media if they ask? (draft a 2-sentence statement)
  d) How do you handle staff communications to prevent panic/leaks?
  e) At what point do you bring law enforcement (Europol / national CERT) in?

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
INJECT 4 – 36 HOURS LATER: RECOVERY PHASE
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Situation at T+36h:
  - Team chose Option B (no payment). Restoration is 60% complete.
  - One restored server (FS-03) re-encrypted 4 hours after restoration.
    This suggests the attacker still has persistence in the environment.
  - The threat actor has published 50 MB of sample data on their leak site.
  - Staff morale is low; 3 IT staff are on sick leave due to stress.
  - Board has requested a full post-incident report within 14 days.

DISCUSSION QUESTIONS – INJECT 4:
  a) What must you do BEFORE re-restoring FS-03 (and the other servers)?
  b) How do you find and remove attacker persistence across 12 servers?
  c) What is the minimum viable "Lessons Learned" report structure?
  d) What long-term security controls would have prevented this attack?
     Prioritise the top 3 by effort vs impact.
  e) The attacker published customer data. What are your obligations to
     affected customers?

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ROLES FOR TABLETOP (assign before starting)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Incident Commander  – coordinates all decisions, owns the bridge
  SOC Lead            – provides technical analysis, owns the SIEM view
  IT Operations       – owns server/network isolation actions
  Legal / Compliance  – GDPR, regulatory notifications, payment legality
  Communications      – internal comms, media, customer notification
  CISO / Management   – resource authorisation, strategic decisions
  External IR (optional) – can be added in Inject 2 as a new participant

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
DEBRIEF CHECKLIST
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

After the exercise, review whether the team:
  [ ] Isolated affected systems within 15 minutes of confirmation
  [ ] Preserved forensic evidence before wiping/restoring
  [ ] Correctly identified the GDPR 72-hour notification trigger and scope
  [ ] Made a documented, justified decision on payment
  [ ] Identified and removed attacker persistence before restoring
  [ ] Produced a crisis communication suitable for customers
  [ ] Identified at least 3 preventive controls for the post-incident report

==============================================================
FACILITATOR NOTES (not for participants):
- Correct answer to Inject 4a: rebuild from CLEAN baseline + change all
  credentials before restoring; persistence was via scheduled task + new
  local admin account created during dwell time.
- GDPR trigger: the 72-hour clock starts when the DPO is formally notified
  of a breach likely to result in risk to individuals – not at discovery.
- Payment legality: in EU, payment to sanctioned entities is prohibited.
  Facilitator may reveal the ransomware group is on EU sanctions list
  after Option A is chosen, to force reconsideration.
