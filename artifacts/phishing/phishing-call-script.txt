SYNTHETIC TRAINING ARTEFACT – NG-SOC LM8 Sub Case 1a
Simulated voice phishing (vishing) call transcript – Activity 2.0.1

SCENARIO: Attacker calls employee's desk phone posing as IT Helpdesk.
Date/Time of simulated call: 2026-06-08 10:05:00 UTC
Caller (attacker): "IT Helpdesk, Michael Carter"
Recipient (victim): Jane Phillips, Finance Department, SecureBank

--- CALL TRANSCRIPT (RECONSTRUCTION FROM VOICEMAIL / WITNESS ACCOUNT) ---

CALLER: "Good morning, this is Michael from the SecureBank IT Security team.
I'm calling about an urgent issue affecting your workstation. Our monitoring
has flagged your machine — employee-ws — as potentially compromised by an
external intrusion attempt this morning."

EMPLOYEE: "Oh — what do I need to do?"

CALLER: "I need to run a quick remote verification check. Can you visit the
following URL in your browser and enter your Windows login credentials so I
can validate your account hasn't been taken over? The address is:
h-t-t-p colon forward-slash forward-slash update-svc dot blackfalcon-sim dot
internal forward-slash verify. It will ask for your username and password."

EMPLOYEE: "Is this the normal IT portal?"

CALLER: "Yes, it's our new verification portal, it went live this week. I can
stay on the line while you do it. It's quite urgent — if we don't verify in the
next 15 minutes your account may be locked as a precaution."

[Employee reportedly provided credentials before ending the call and checking
with a colleague, at which point she recognised it as suspicious and contacted
the SOC.]

--- END TRANSCRIPT ---

OBSERVABLE ANALYSIS FOR EXERCISE:
  Type: Vishing (voice phishing)
  Pretext: IT Helpdesk impersonation
  Technique: Authority + urgency + credential harvesting URL
  URL delivered verbally: http://update-svc.blackfalcon-sim.internal/verify
  Resolves to: 10.10.20.20 (C2 server, within sandbox only)
  Credentials targeted: Windows AD credentials
  MITRE ATT&CK: T1566 (Phishing), T1598.004 (Vishing)
  Result: Employee provisionally provided credentials (scenario context)

NOTES FOR INSTRUCTOR:
  Students read this transcript and triage it alongside the email and SMS.
  Key learning: multi-channel attacks are coordinated; the same C2 domain
  appears across email, SMS and voice call — a strong corroborating IoC.
  Students should add the phone number and verbal URL to their IoC list.
